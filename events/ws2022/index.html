LEAD-ME Winter Training School Lisbon 2023

Accessible Embodied Interaction

16-17 March 2023

Faculdade de Ciências da Universidade de Lisboa

Check the Programme of the LEAD-ME Winter School 2023

View the video playlist on the LEAD-ME Youtube Channel

Organisers

Introduction

The LEAD-ME Winter Training School Lisbon 2023 will take place in Lisbon in early 2023. LASIGE and ETIC will jointly organise it. The school will offer lectures and practical training on the topic of accessible embodied interaction. Embodied interaction can represent an alternative to traditional means of interaction with digital media and other resources. This school will explore the use of biosignals or eye tracking as an input medium that can improve the accessibility of, for example, virtual environments. Trainees will also get to ideate and work on their own solutions in a full day hackathon style session. To participate in the hackathon, trainees are expected to bring their own computer. Other materials will be made available on site.

We invite participants from the industry and academia, both early-career researchers and seasoned researchers who are interested in exploring how embodied interaction can contribute to accessible media.

The Winter Training school will be a hybrid event, with the option to participate in the first day both face-to-face and online. It is free of charge, but requires pre-registration. Some scholarships are available from LEAD ME to cover traveling and accommodation. The max number of funded participants is 15, and the places will be assigned on a first come first served basis.

How to apply for the Winter School

Email the application to Carlos Duarte and Mónica Pedro. Your application should include your CV and an application letter stating your motivation to participate in the Winter School.

Instructions on how to register for the Winter School will be provided in the response to the application.

Funded by the Horizon 2020 Framework Programme of the European Union
