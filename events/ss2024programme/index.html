LEAD-ME Summer Training School Oslo 2024 Programme

Accessibility in Digital Learning Resources

September 16-17 2024

Venue: Oslo

Program

Monday 16th September

Time
09:00 - 09:30Introduction to the two days of training
09:45 - 10:30Keynote speech: “Bridging the The accessibility gap by AI”
10:30 - 10:50Coffee break
10:50 - 11:10Introduction of four users demonstrate how they operate, perceive and understand digital learning resources
11:20 - 12:00Parallel hands-on stations where users with different needs will demonstrate
12:00 - 13:00Lunch
13:00 - 13:30“One size fits all or one size fits one?” – Accessibility through preferences
13:30 - 15:00Industry challenge 1: Pictures, videos etc for blind and visually impaired
13:30 - 15:00Industry challenge 2: Navigation
15:00 - 16:00Presenting results of Day 1, discussion, feedback, next steps
16:00 - 17:00Roof-top drinks if sunny
18:00 - 22:00Dinner with free seating

Tuesday 17th September

Time
09:00 - 09:30Mingling with coffee
10:30 - 10:45Introduction of the four users
11:20 - 12:00Parallel hands-on stations where users with different needs will demonstrate
12:00 - 13:00Lunch
13:00 - 15:00Common industry challenge for all groups: Balancing Native Accessibility Standards and Personalized Features
15:00 - 16:00Pitching with jury
16:00 - 17:00Wrap-up and mingling

Program Details

Day 1: Navigate and perceive

09.00–09.30: Introduction to the two days of training.

09.30–11.30: User demonstrations

  • Four users demonstrate how they navigate and perceive digital learning resources – different age groups. 5 to 7 PhD students on each demo “station” Using the Digjobb.no as partner.

11.30–12.30: Lunch

12.30–13.45: How to navigate, perceive (WCAG principles) a digital learning resource Navigation break-out group 1 (10-14 persons)

  • How to interpret WCAG/WAD in relation to eye-gaze navigation, one-button, two-button, braille, deaf. Different PhD-specializations in every group

14.00–15:00: How to navigate, perceive (WCAG principles) a digital learning resource Navigation break-out group 2 (10-14 persons)

  • How to interpret WCAG/WAD in relation to eye-gaze navigation, one-button, two-button, braille, deaf. One facilitator for each group

15.00–16.00: Pitching with experts

Day 2: Understand

09.00–09.30: Understandable (WCAG principle)

  • How to understand content – dyslexia, symbol users, cognitive challenges.

09.30–11.30: User demonstrations

  • Four users demonstrate how they use learning resources – different age groups.
  • Five to seven PhD students on each demonstration “station” Using the Digjobb.no as partner.

11.30–12.30: Lunch

12.30–13.45: Navigation break-out group 1 (10-14 persons)

  • How to interpret WCAG/WAD in relation to dyslexia, symbol users, cognitive challenges ... Different PhD-specializations in every group

14.00–15.00: Navigation break-out group 2 (10-14 persons)

  • How to interpret WCAG/WAD in relation to dyslexia, symbol users, cognitive challenges ... Different PhD-specializations in every group

15.00–16.00: Pitching with jury
